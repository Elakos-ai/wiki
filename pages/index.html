I am a big fan of detective series, especially those that focus on psychology, intelligence, and the inner struggles of the characters. Some of my favorites are Dexter , The Mentalist, and YOU . What appeals to me about Dexter is the unique protagonist, a serial killer who works as a blood expert for the police. His double life, strict moral code, and struggle with his inner demons make the plot incredibly gripping. I love watching him balance between the darkness inside him and the desire to be “normal.”


In The Mentalist, I really like Patrick Jane>, a man with incredible intelligence and observation skills who uses his skills to solve crimes. His methods are unconventional, his charisma is strong, and his personal tragedy adds depth to everything.


I watch YOU because of the unusual presentation - the story is told from the perspective of a man who seems charming and scary at the same time. I'm interested in the topic of boundaries, manipulation, and how easily a person can justify even the darkest actions if he believes that he is doing it "in the name of love." Such series hook me because they are not just about crimes. They are about the inner world of people, their secrets, weaknesses, strength of mind, and struggle with themselves. I love stories where each character is not black and white, but multi-layered, and where every action has consequences.
